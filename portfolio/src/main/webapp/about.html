Hello! My name is Nathan Safir. I was born in Chicago (go 'hawks!), but grew in Kansas City. When I was 16, my family moved to the Netherlands, and I later graduated high school from the American School of the Hague. From there, I went to the University of Georgia as a Foundation Fellow. The Foundation Fellowship has given me several incredible experiences, from studying computing ethics at Oxford over a Maymester, travelling internationally and to New York and DC during spring break, and networking with an amazing community of alumni and peers.

At UGA, I am pursuing majors in computer science and geography. I aim to graduate with a B.S./M.S. in Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence and a B.A. in Geography. I have always enjoyed problem solving and mathematics, so when first learned about machine learning, I was instantly hooked. I am especially interested in deep learning, generative modeling, and representational learning. I hope to have a career where I can work on using machine learning for social good.

Outside of academics my interests include ice hockey (I'm currently coaching a youth team in Athens, GA), reading (follow me on Goodreads!), and trivia (fun fact -- my roomates and I have made over $500 in gift cards from weekly trivia at a local piza shop).
